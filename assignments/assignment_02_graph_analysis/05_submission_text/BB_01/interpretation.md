## 7. Component and Cross-Floor Limitations

This dataset contains **2 disconnected component(s)**, one per physical floor.

**Why floors are disconnected:**  
The Assignment 1 graph generation exported only horizontal (bbox) spatial adjacencies.
Stair and lift cells are present as `core` nodes but share no vertical edges with
equivalent nodes on adjacent floors. This is a modelling boundary, not a building defect.

**Implications for metric interpretation:**

- Betweenness and diameter were computed **per component** to avoid cross-component distortion.
- Closeness uses Wasserman-Faust normalization, which scales each node by its reachable fraction.
- All results in sections 2–6 describe **within-floor** spatial relationships only.

## 9. Adjacency-Threshold Limitation

The edges in this graph are derived from **bounding-box spatial adjacency**
(gap threshold: 0.45 m), not from door aperture positions.

**Implications:**

- Two rooms sharing a wall are connected regardless of whether a door exists between them.
- The graph over-estimates accessibility between spaces that are adjacent but separated by
  solid walls (e.g., two apartments sharing a party wall).
- Centrality metrics in sections 2–5 reflect geometric proximity, not traversal through openings.
